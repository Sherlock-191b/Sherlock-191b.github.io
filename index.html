About

I am a physics graduate with a Master’s in Physics (Applied Electronics) from the University of Kerala, with research interests spanning quantum many-body systems, attosecond physics, and experimental laser–optics platforms. My work bridges theoretical modelling, numerical simulations, and experimental spectroscopy, driven by the pursuit of understanding the fundamental nature of matter through advanced instrumentation.

My academic path has combined space science, condensed matter physics, and quantum systems, resulting in a versatile skill set:

  • Experimental: FTIR spectroscopy, XRD analysis, UV–Vis spectroscopy, Michelson interferometry, Zeeman effect, diffraction experiments, electronic circuit design, CRO/DSO operation, and meteorite sample preparation.
  • Computational & Theoretical: Python (data analysis and simulations), Mathematica (symbolic computation), C/C++, Qiskit (quantum computing), similarity matrix generation, and solutions of Fokker–Planck equations.
  • Interdisciplinary Research: Mid-infrared asteroid–meteorite spectral matching and modelling active particle dynamics in viscoelastic baths.

I have qualified IIT JAM (AIR 876), collaborated with the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad for my Master’s dissertation, and presented work at workshops hosted by institutions such as DST, IUCAA and VSSC.

My long-term goal is to experimentally probe and manipulate correlated, topological, and low-dimensional systems using state-of-the-art laser-based techniques, precision spectroscopy, and advanced condensed-matter instrumentation. I am particularly motivated by the possibility of uncovering new quasiparticle behavior, light-engineered quantum states, and nonequilibrium phenomena that can reshape our understanding of quantum matter.

Research Experience

Research Internship — Generalized Fokker–Planck Equation for Active Ornstein–Uhlenbeck Particles

University of Kerala · Jun 2025 – Sep 2025

Developed a generalized Fokker–Planck framework incorporating inertia, non-Markovian memory, external fields, and viscoelastic effects. Conducted analytical modelling and numerical simulations using Mathematica and Python.

Master’s Thesis — Spectral Insights into Carbonaceous Chondrites Origins

Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad · Mar 2025 – Jun 2025

Built a quantitative spectral similarity framework using Spitzer IRS and FTIR meteorite spectra to correlate main-belt asteroids with CV chondrites. Developed Python tools for thermal modelling, spectral stitching, and weighted similarity analysis.

Bachelor’s Project — Discrete Transistor-Based Logic Gates

University of Kerala · Feb 2022 – Apr 2022

Designed, simulated, and fabricated transistor-based logic gates using BC547 NPN transistors, including PCB layout and validation of all seven basic logic gates.
