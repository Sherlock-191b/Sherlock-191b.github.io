About

I am a Master’s graduate in Physics with a strong interest in quantum materials under ultrafast and nonequilibrium excitation. My academic focus lies in understanding how electron dynamics, defects, and correlations manifest on femtosecond and attosecond timescales, enabling access to transient phases and emergent quasiparticle behavior inaccessible in equilibrium.

My training spans statistical physics, spectroscopy, and computational data analysis, with hands-on experience in laser-based and spectroscopic techniques as well as theoretical modeling and simulation. I aim to pursue doctoral research at the intersection of quantum materials, ultrafast science, and precision condensed-matter experimentation.

Research Interests

  • Ultrafast and attosecond dynamics in quantum materials
  • Nonequilibrium condensed matter physics
  • Defects, correlations, and quasiparticle phenomena
  • Light–matter interaction and advanced spectroscopy

Research Experience

Research Internship — Active Matter Theory

University of Kerala · Jun 2025 – Sep 2025

Constructed a generalized Fokker–Planck framework for active Ornstein–Uhlenbeck particles incorporating inertia, non-Markovian memory kernels, external fields, and viscoelastic effects. Performed analytical modeling and numerical simulations using Mathematica and Python.

Master’s Thesis — Planetary Spectroscopy

Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad · Mar 2025 – Jun 2025

Developed a quantitative spectral similarity framework using Spitzer IRS and FTIR meteorite spectra to correlate main-belt asteroids with CV chondrites. Built Python-based tools for thermal modeling, spectral stitching, and weighted similarity analysis.
