About

I am a physics graduate with a Master’s in Physics (Applied Electronics) from the University of Kerala, with research interests spanning quantum many-body systems, attosecond physics, and experimental laser–optics platforms. My work bridges theoretical modelling, numerical simulations, and experimental spectroscopy, and I am motivated by the pursuit of understanding the fundamental nature of matter through advanced instrumentation and experimental setups.

My academic path has combined space science, condensed matter physics, and quantum systems, giving me a versatile skill set: Experimental – FTIR spectroscopy, XRD analysis, UV-Vis spectroscopy, Michelson interferometry, Zeeman effect, diffraction experiments, electronic circuit design, CRO/DSO operation, and meteorite sample preparation. Computational & Theoretical – Python (data analysis, simulations), Mathematica (symbolic computation), C/C++, Qiskit (quantum computing), similarity matrix generation, solving Fokker–Planck equations for complex systems. Interdisciplinary Research – From mid-infrared asteroid–meteorite spectral matching to modelling active particle dynamics in viscoelastic baths. I have qualified IIT JAM (AIR 876), collaborated with Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad for my Master’s dissertation, and presented work in various workshops hosted by institutions such as IUCAA and VSSC. My career goal is to advance into experimental quantum optics and ultracold atom research, with a strong interest in large-scale precision setups that probe the quantum nature of matter. I am preparing for applications to leading research institutes like the Max Planck Institute of Quantum Optics, aiming to contribute to cutting-edge developments in fundamental physics.

Research Experience

Research Internship — Active Matter Theory

University of Kerala · Jun 2025 – Sep 2025

Constructed a generalized Fokker–Planck framework for active Ornstein–Uhlenbeck particles incorporating inertia, non-Markovian memory kernels, external fields, and viscoelastic effects. Performed analytical modeling and numerical simulations using Mathematica and Python.

Master’s Thesis — Planetary Spectroscopy

Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad · Mar 2025 – Jun 2025

Developed a quantitative spectral similarity framework using Spitzer IRS and FTIR meteorite spectra to correlate main-belt asteroids with CV chondrites. Built Python-based tools for thermal modeling, spectral stitching, and weighted similarity analysis.
